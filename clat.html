CLAT Exam Information

What is CLAT?

CLAT, or Common Law Admission Test, is a centralized national level entrance exam for admission to undergraduate (LLB) and postgraduate (LLM) law programs offered by participating National Law Universities (NLUs) across India.

Important Dates

  • Notification Release Date: Usually in December
  • Application Start Date: Usually in January
  • Application End Date: Usually in March
  • CLAT Exam Date: Usually in May
  • Result Declaration: Usually in June

Eligibility Criteria

  • For UG (LLB): Candidates must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with a minimum of 45% marks (40% for SC/ST candidates).
  • For PG (LLM): Candidates must have completed LLB or equivalent degree with a minimum of 50% marks (45% for SC/ST candidates).
  • There is no upper age limit for appearing in CLAT.

Exam Pattern

The CLAT exam consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) from subjects such as English, General Knowledge, Legal Aptitude, Logical Reasoning, and Elementary Mathematics (for UG).

Cutoff Marks

The cutoff marks for CLAT vary each year and depend on factors such as the difficulty level of the exam, the number of candidates appearing, and the seats available in NLUs.

Gender Ratio

The participation of girls in CLAT has been increasing steadily. However, the ratio of boys to girls varies across NLUs and depends on several factors including location and facilities offered.
