NEET Exam Information

What is NEET?

NEET, or National Eligibility cum Entrance Test, is a national-level entrance examination conducted in India for admission to undergraduate medical courses (MBBS/BDS) in government and private medical colleges.

Important Dates

  • Registration Start Date: Usually in November
  • Registration End Date: Usually in December
  • NEET Exam Date: Usually in May
  • Result Declaration: Usually in June

Eligibility Criteria

  • Age: Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission or will complete the age on or before 31st December of the year of admission.
  • Qualification: Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.
  • Marks: General category candidates must have scored at least 50% marks in PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology) aggregate in their qualifying examination. For SC/ST/OBC candidates, the minimum aggregate is 40%.

Exam Pattern

The NEET exam consists of one paper containing 180 multiple-choice questions (MCQs) from Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology.

Cutoff Marks

The cutoff marks for NEET vary each year and are determined based on factors such as the number of candidates appearing, difficulty level of the exam, and availability of seats in medical colleges.

Girl Ratio

The ratio of girls appearing for NEET has been increasing steadily over the years. However, the number of girls opting for medical courses still remains slightly lower compared to boys.
